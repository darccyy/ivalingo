# Ivalingo

Ivalingo /ivaˈliŋgo/ is an International Auxillary Language modelled after Esperanto.

- Phonemic inventory: Extension of Toki Pona, inspired by Esperanto and other IALs
- Phonotactics: From scratch, based loosely on Esperanto
- Grammar (and basic vocab): From Esperanto
- Vocabulary: From [source languages](#source-languages)

# Phonotactics

## Syllable Structure

$\langle ((s|c) C) V (n|m|l) \rangle$ - Simplified: ❬(C)(C)V(C)❭

1. ❬V❭ includes diphthongs (❬w,j❭ are included in vowel)
2. No [repeated consonants](#repeated-consonants)
3. ❬C❭ is optional word-initially or between ❬i❭ and ❬a❭
4. ❬C❭ includes all consonants, with ❬w,j❭ only word-initially
5. ❬(s,c)❭ cannot appear between non-vocalic consonants
6. ❬(s,c)❭ can only appear before ❬p,t,k,f,w,l❭
7. ❬n❭ cannot appear before ❬p,b❭ (Use ❬m❭), and ❬m❭ cannot appear before ❬t,d,s,z,f,v❭ (Use ❬n❭)
8. ❬n,m,l❭ cannot appear before ❬w,j❭

## Repeated Consonants

No repeated consonants are valid.

For affixes, separate with ❬a❭ if required.

- Eg. ❬mal + longa❭ -> ❬malalonga❭

For compound words, separate with appropriate [PoS](#parts-of-speech) suffix.

- Eg. ❬viv + vespero❭ (vespero de viv**o**) -> ❬vivovespero❭

## Repeated Vowels

No repeated vowels are valid, except ❬ia❭ (including diphthongs ❬iaj❭, ❬iaw❭)

## Stress

Penultimate syllable (Second last).
Eg. ❬ivalingo❭ = ❬iva**LIN**go❭

## Affirmative and Negative

- Yes, Jes = ❬si❭
- No, Ne = ❬ne❭

## Interrogative particle (ĉu)

There are two interrogative particles, ❬cu❭ for closed (yes/no) questions, and ❬cun❭ for open question.
This is usually expressed with tone in English.
Both are translated as 'ĉu' in Esperanto.

Eg:

- Closed question
- - `en` Do you like blue or red? Yes / No
- - `eo` Ĉu vi ŝatas blua aŭ ruĝa? Jes / Ne
- - `iv` cu vi catas blua aw ruza? Si / Ne
- Open question
- - `en` Do you like blue or red? Blue / Red
- - `eo` Ĉu vi ŝatas blua aŭ ruĝa? Blua / Ruĝa
- - `iv` cun vi catas blua aw ruza? Blua / Ruza

## Correlatives

- Generality: ajn (after word)
- - Eg. `en` whatever, `eo` kio ajn, `iv` iko ajn
- Proximity: t(i)-
- - Eg. `en` this (thing), `eo` tio, `iv` tato
- Distance: fol (after word)
- - Eg. `en` yonder, `eo` tie for, `iv` ate fol
- Association: de (before word)
- - Eg. `en` whose, `eo` kies, `iv` de kiulo (of who)

## Prefix

- Interrogative, Relative: ik-
- Indication: at-
- Indefinite: iv-
- Universal: af-
- Negative: nen-
- Other: al-

## Country, Language Names

All use endonyms

- Country name: -o
- - `en` England
- - `eo` Anglio
- - `iv` Inglo

- Person in country (living / citizen): -ano
- - `en` English person (living in England
- - `eo` anglo
- - `iv` Inglano

- Person from country (born): -ulo [remove?]
- - `en` English person (from England)
- - `eo` anglo
- - `iv` Inglulo

- Quality of country: -a
- - `en` English (adjective)
- - `eo` angla
- - `iv` Ingla

Language name not derived from country

- Language: -o
- - `en` English
- - `eo` la angla
- - `iv` Inglico (from `en` English, **not** `en` England)

- Quality of language: -a
- - `en` The English language
- - `eo` la angla lingvo
- - `iv` Inglica lingo

- Speaker of language: -anto
- - `en` English speaker
- - `eo` anglolingvano
- - `iv` Inglicanto
